Who are we?

Treat a tree is a non-profit organization that is dedicated to fight deforestation by planting trees and educate about the environment therefore trying to make a better future for the planet and humanity.


Our mission and Values

Our values are to make a more sustainable life and protect the environment. These are critical times for the environment as we know more about the consequences of our past actions, so we must act and do what we can to heal the planet.

WHY PLANTING A TREE ?:

TODAY, 80% OF THE WORLD'S FORESTS HAVE BEEN ALREADY DESTROYED.

Deforestation is mainly caused by: fires, clear-cutting for agriculture, ranching and development, unsustainable logging for timber and degradation due to climate change are withing the main reasons.

Forgetting the importance of trees is making our eco system in danger. It is by the help of trees that our water cycle is working: they ground the water in their roots and release it into the atmosphere. A good example is the Amazon forest where 50% of the water is treated by trees and plants.

It is prooved that forest gardens is fighting deforestation more than everything. Farmers has to decrease their dependence on forests for food, timber and other industrial products as such practices of burning clearing and plowing are increasing considerably global warming.

Moreover, it is in the forest that live a large number of animal species. By destroying their habitat, we are leading to their extinction. It is not only a tragedy for our biodiversity but has also consequences for local populations who is needing this biodiversity such as for hunting or medical researches.

Brazil

The Atlantic Forest, also known as Mata Atlantica, once covered 130 million hectares across Brazil. Now, this tropical rainforest ecosystem is home to 70% of the country’s population. Centuries of deforestation for timber, sugar cane, coffee, cattle ranching, and urban sprawl have reduced the size of the forest by over 90%. Although the water supply for Brazil’s two most populous cities (Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo) comes from this forest, there is a lack of public policies and resources to protect it.

Tanzania

Mount Kilimanjaro – the “rooftop of Africa” – is the highest freestanding mountain in the world. The mountain’s forests are a vital source of water for the surrounding region, feeding into one of Tanzania’s major rivers, the Pangani. These forests are declining at a rapid rate, as millions of trees are cut for firewood, charcoal, and agriculture each year. Recent studies show that this has a direct effect on Kilimanjaro’s glaciers and rainfall patterns in the region. Planting trees here will help restore balance to the climate and combat poverty in rural communities through the use of a brand new tree-tracking mobile app.

USA

California's forests provide innumerable benefits, including clean water and air, recreation, timber, habitat, and beautiful scenery. Healthy forests also play an important role in addressing climate change. Five years of drought and a large-scale bark beetle infestation have seriously damaged California’s forests. 2017’s record-breaking wildfire season burned more than 1.3 million acres – an area the size of Delaware. Now, a record 129 million trees need to be restored in California.

Vietnam

Vietnam’s tropical forests support rich biodiversity and a precious, sensitive ecosystem. 25 million people depend on forest products for subsistence. Over-exploitation of the land has endangered many native species of plants and animals, and increased the impact of natural disasters. Planting trees here will help conserve threatened species, regulate climate, provide economic stability for local villagers, and protect watershed areas that serve the community on a daily basis.
